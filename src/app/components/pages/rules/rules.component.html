Rules

Shiny Pokémon of the Fortnight Official Rules

General

  1. Each competition typically runs from 00:00 (12:00am) on Sunday to 23:59 (11:59pm) on Saturday two weeks later for a total of 14 days. (It STARTS at the beginning of Sunday and ENDS at the end of Saturday)
  2. Competition start and end time is based in Pacific Standard Time (PST).
  3. Participants can hunt in any main series game… and Pokémon Go too.
  4. Events with boosted rates (promoted raids, events*) will count toward points, at the discretion of the most recent fortnight's champion.
  5. Participants can use any hunting method available (random encounters, run aways, catch combo, horde hunting, poke radar, SOS, "murder method", masuda method, soft resetting, wormholes, dynamax adventures, raiding)
  6. Hacking, cloning, swap breeding, knowingly entering raid dens where you're certain to encounter a shiny, guaranteed static encounters** — all of these are examples of not competing fairly within the competition. No exploits, known or unknown, should be utilised for your hunt. If you're uncertain if your method is considered legitimate, ask the channel.
  7. Hunting with external hardware (controllers with turbo functions, Switch Up, etc.) is considered illegal as it does not require the hunter's physical presence to get their target. The Pokémon GO Plus+, and other devices that auto-catch in GO, are allowed.
  8. If you are doing a Masuda hunt, you cannot start gathering eggs until the theme formally starts. The hunt begins at midnight and gathering eggs is viewed as part of the hunt. The same is true for starting a radar chain or beginning an SOS chain.
  9. Use of Shiny Charm and Masuda Method are encouraged. If you do not have a shiny charm, we will be happy to help you get one.
  10. Any disagreement over the rules will be settled with either polite discussion in Slack, or a duel to the death.

Scoring

  1. Each user is to post screenshot/photographic proof of having caught the Pokémon within the allotted time period. A successful shiny caught will result in one (1) point.
  2. Two tracks of points are recorded. A single point for any successful hunt within a theme, and a total of all points scored within the themes.
  3. For the total points track, you may (typically) score as many points as would allow unique evolutions/forms/visible gender differences within the theme. e.g. If you catch three Lillipup, that would give three points, as you could evolve them into Herdier and Stoutland. If you catch three shiny Stoutland, you would get only one point, as you could not turn these into three unique species/forms.
  4. The person who selects the theme can put additional restrictions on the above rule if needed for the theme.
  5. Unless otherwise specified, any pokémon that has the potential to become a winning point does not need to be evolved into the winning species to claim the point. This is also subject to restrictions by the person choosing the theme.
  6. The last fortnight of the year is a “Free for All” where all Pokémon are worth 1 point. Each unique Pokémon evolution/forme/visibly distinct gender is worth 1 point.***
  7. Only catches made in the competition period will count.

Verification

  1. All catches must come with screenshot/photographic proof. This should be posted directly to the #shinyhunting channel and not to an external service.
  2. Your in game name must be visible.
  3. Date of capture must be visible and fall within the period of the current competition (adjusted for timezone).

Theme Selection

  1. The winner of the previous competition gets to pick the theme for the next competition.
  2. Themes should allow for a variety of Pokémon to be caught. Many of us have a lot of shinies already and it's more fun to have the option to catch something new.
  3. Themes that are relevant to real life events are encouraged (Spooky theme at Halloween etc).
  4. Ideally, there should be clear criteria for exactly which Pokémon count toward the theme, either based on specific Pokémon list or a clearly identifiable attribute like type. e.g. a Spooky theme could be any ghost type, but a “Food” theme would need a specific list of qualifying Pokémon.
  5. If you're really stuck for a theme, feel free to ask for help. If you're not going to be available, you can delegate theme selection to another player.
  6. Themes should be chosen to provide maximum enjoyment rather than difficulty or frustration.
  7. The person choosing the theme is not allowed to “win” their own theme. If they are the first person to score a point, the next person to score will be allowed to pick the next theme.

Conditionals

  1. If a winner does not select a theme by the start of the next fortnight OR nobody gets a point in a given fortnight, a run-off will occur.
  2. The first person to catch a shiny Pokémon, same rules as above, during this run-off, selects the theme for the fortnight.
  3. This immediately ends the run-off, and once the list for the theme is announced, the fortnight is considered to have begun. The person who ends the run-off does not get a point.
*Promoted shinies caught on Community Day in Pokémon Go are not worth points.
